All opinions are based on hands-on testing and independent research. --- Is WPS Office good for small teams? Yes. WPS Office is a strong, budget-friendly option for freelancers, solopreneurs, and teams of 2–10 people who need solid Microsoft Office compatibility without the high cost of Microsoft 365. Its free tier covers basic document editing, while the affordable Premium plan adds AI tools, more cloud storage, and ad removal. Features Overview

WPS Office bundles three core modules — Writer, Spreadsheets, and Presentation — that closely mirror Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. The interface uses a ribbon-style layout that feels immediately familiar to anyone switching from Microsoft Office. Key highlights:
  • Full .docx, .xlsx, and .pptx read/write support
  • Built-in PDF reader, editor, and converter
  • Professional template library (1,000+ designs)
  • OCR for scanned documents (Premium)
  • Screen recording and form-fill tools
  • Polished mobile apps for iOS and Android with offline editing
For a small team that needs to open, edit, and share Office files daily, WPS covers the basics and then some — especially at its price point. ---

Collaboration Tools

WPS supports real-time co-editing through WPS Cloud. Multiple users can work on the same document, spreadsheet, or presentation with:
  • Link sharing with view/edit permissions
  • Inline comments and @mentions
  • Track changes with accept/reject
  • Version history (Premium)
The honest assessment: Collaboration works, but it is not as fluid as Google Docs or Microsoft 365. Simultaneous editing with 2–3 people is fine. With 5+ people in the same document, you may notice lag or sync delays. If your team collaborates in real-time all day, Google Workspace remains the better choice. If you mostly work independently and share files for review, WPS is perfectly adequate. ---

Compatibility with Microsoft Office Formats

This is WPS Office's biggest strength. Compatibility with .docx, .xlsx, and .pptx is very good:
  • Word documents: Basic and intermediate formatting preserves correctly 95%+ of the time
  • Excel spreadsheets: Standard formulas and charts work well; complex VBA macros may need adjustment
  • PowerPoint decks: Layouts, transitions, and embedded media carry over with minimal issues
For most small-team workflows — proposals, invoices, reports, budget trackers, pitch decks — WPS handles the format faithfully. The occasional layout shift in heavily formatted files is the main caveat, and it happens far less often than with LibreOffice. ---

AI Features (WPS AI)

WPS AI is included with Premium and covers:
  • Text generation: Draft emails, proposals, and reports from prompts
  • Summarization: Condense long documents or PDFs into key points
  • Translation: Translate content within documents
  • Grammar and style: Catch errors and improve readability
  • Chart creation: Generate charts from text descriptions
  • PDF Q&A: Ask questions about PDF content
Our take: WPS AI is helpful for everyday tasks — drafting a quick proposal, summarizing meeting notes, or cleaning up grammar. It is not as powerful as Microsoft Copilot (which benefits from GPT-4 class models and deep Office integration), but it is included in the Premium price with no extra subscription. For a small team that wants AI assistance without another $20–30/month bill, this is solid value. ---

Cloud Storage

  • Free: 1 GB WPS Cloud
  • Premium: 20 GB (expandable)
  • Integration: Can open/save to Dropbox, Google Drive, and OneDrive
WPS Cloud is functional but basic — it handles sync and sharing well enough but lacks the deep folder collaboration and permissions of Google Drive or OneDrive. Many teams will prefer to save directly to their existing cloud provider, which WPS supports. ---

Templates

WPS offers over 1,000 professionally designed templates across categories:
  • Business plans and proposals
  • Invoices and financial trackers
  • Resumes and CVs
  • Marketing presentations
  • Social media graphics
Templates are regularly updated and many are genuinely well-designed — a notable advantage over LibreOffice's more dated options. ---

Verdict

Best for

  • Freelancers and solopreneurs replacing Microsoft 365 on a tight budget
  • Small teams (2–10) that work mostly offline or in hybrid setups
  • Users who need strong .docx/.xlsx/.pptx compatibility and PDF tools
  • Teams wanting built-in AI without a separate subscription

Skip if

  • Your team requires seamless real-time co-editing with external clients daily
  • You are already deeply invested in the Google Workspace ecosystem
  • You need enterprise-grade admin controls, compliance, or DLP features
  • Your workflows depend on complex VBA macros or Excel add-ins

Alternatives

Google Workspace

Best-in-class real-time collaboration and sharing. If your team already uses Gmail and needs external client editing, this is the safer choice. The tradeoff: significantly higher cost per user and weaker offline support.

Microsoft 365

The gold standard for compatibility and advanced features. Includes Teams, advanced security, and the strongest AI (Copilot). Choose this if your budget allows and you need maximum Office fidelity. For a 5-person team, expect to pay $360+/year vs $50 for WPS.

LibreOffice

Completely free and open-source with strong desktop features and full offline use. The downsides: no cloud collaboration, no AI features, weaker Office compatibility, and a dated interface. Best for privacy-focused users who work entirely offline.

OnlyOffice

Lightweight suite with good MS Office compatibility and self-hosted collaboration options. A good middle ground between WPS and LibreOffice, though its cloud offering is less mature and the template library is smaller.

Free/Cheaper Alternatives Note

LibreOffice remains the best zero-cost desktop option. Google's free consumer accounts work for light collaboration. OnlyOffice Community Edition offers free self-hosted collaboration for technically inclined teams. ---
